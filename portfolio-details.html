  • Category: Fiscal Policy
  • Data Source: MOF (Egypt) Dec 2024 Financial Report
  • Project date: 15 January, 2025
  • Project URL: GitHub Repository

Main Budget Operations:
Strategic Guidance Insights (FY15-25)

The project aims to recommend policy priorities based on main budget operations indicators.

The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly:
At a glance, one can see that the overal budtget deficit (as a % of GDP) has improved over the study period. In FY25, it stands at -7.3% of GDP, down from -12.5% in FY16. The primary deficit (as a % of GDP) has also improved over the study period. Its values turned positive in FY18 and has rose to 3.5% of GDP. Comparing the two values indicate that Egypt may have an issue with interest payments. Meanwhile, expenditure as a % of GDP has declined, but revenue as a % of GDP has also declined. This shows Egypt recent fiscal consolidation efforts but also suggests high correlation between expenditures and revnues; i.e., high impact of expenditures on collected revnues and how state spending influences the economy.

Expenditure Decomposition:
An examination of the top 4 contributing factors shows that interest payments have become the largest component of total expenditures at 1.8 Trillion EGP in FY25 compared to 0.2 in FY15. This rise has beaten any other increase in compensation for state employees, subsidies and social benefits, and purchases of non-financial assets. Non-financial assets purchases reflect the state's investment in infrastructure and other assets.

Revenue Decomposition:
Revnues have increased as a nominal value from 0.5 trillion EGP to 2.6 trillion EGP over the study period. This was influenced by a corresponding increase in income tax and value added taxes revenues from 0.1 and 0.1 trillion EGP to 0.8 and 0.8 trillion EGP, each. Property taxes have also increased marginally over the past two financial years reflecting the state's efforts in updating its tax system. Non-tax revnues has seen a more moderate increase especially in the last two FYs. This is especially reflected in proceeds from sales of goods and services which standing at 0.4 and 0.3 trillion EGP in FY24 and FY25, respectively. That indicator is boosted by the recent transaction concerning "Ras el Hekma" land sale.

Policy Recommendations:
The state should continue its fiscal consolidation efforts. This time around, it should focus on reducing interest payments as a % of GDP as its first priority. Otherwise, it risks falling into a debt trap. Realistically speaking, it's not possible for the state to continue land sales to increase revenues and support the budget deficit indefinitely. It should focus on increasing tax revenues via expanding the tax base and increasing tax compliance. Finally, the private sector needs to be encouraged to invest in the country to increase economic activity. This would allow the state to switch focus to tax compliance instead of non-tax revenues. However, this is highly dependent on the geopolitics at play in the Palastine-Israel conflict and the state's ability to maintain a stable political environment.
